The Rope Ladder of Explanation
Where description turns into explanation — and explanation into meta-interpretation
Theorist climbing; empirical adjudication stabilising each rung.

Scientific understanding is not a staircase but a rope ladder disappearing into mist. The climber ascends by stabilising one rung at a time. The court below does not provide a summit — it decides whether the rung just climbed will hold.

Description organises appearances into reportable facts and stable patterns.

Explanation begins when a quantitative relation constrains outcomes within a defined domain description → explanation. It becomes determinate only when constitutive relations and boundary conditions close the system.

Meta-interpretation begins when we explain why whole families of laws share structure explanation → meta.

# Rung Triad Verb Contribution
1 Percept Notice | Detect Raw encounter.
2 Fact Name | Classify Shared description.
3 Regularity Generalise | Correlate Patterned behaviour.
4 Operational Law Explain | Predict Quantitative constraint within a regime.
5 Constitutive Closure Specify | Close Equations of state + conditions make prediction determinate.
6 Structural Principle Constrain | Derive Why admissible laws must have this form.
7 Unifying Framework Interpret | Re-express Meta-level coherence across domains.
Explanation is not a fundamental equation alone. It is constraint plus constitutive closure, continuously tested by empirical adjudication.
